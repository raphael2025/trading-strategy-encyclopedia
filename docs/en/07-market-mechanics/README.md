# 07 · Market Mechanics

How large players actually move price — and the crucial difference between a mechanism being **real** and being **tradeable**. These phenomena genuinely happen; the honest finding is that "spot it and time your entry off it" does not survive look-ahead, placebo, and cost for a retail trader. Their value is **defensive** (where *not* to put a stop, why to keep leverage low), not predictive.

| Topic | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| [Liquidity Hunts](liquidity-hunts.md) | ⚠️ real / ❌ as timing | Liquidation cascades and order pools are real; sweep-fade entries don't beat placebo and bleed on intraday cost. Hindsight labels. |
| [Stop Hunting](stop-hunting.md) | ⚠️ real / ❌ as timing | Stops do get tagged — useful defense — but "trade the hunt" is the falsified liquidity-sweep fade. |
| [Market Manipulation](market-manipulation.md) | ⚠️ real / ❌ as signal | Spoofing/wash/P&D/cornering are documented and illegal; profitable detection is HFT-latency or post-hoc forensics, not a retail edge. |
| [Wyckoff Accumulation & Distribution](wyckoff-accumulation-distribution.md) | ❌ as timing / ⚠️ as lens | Phases only knowable in hindsight; subjective labeling overfits; entry levels are crowded liquidity. |

The unifying lesson: **mechanism ≠ edge.** Knowing how a liquidation cascade or a spoofed wall works makes you a better *defender* (size down near liquidation clusters, don't stop at the obvious level, stay low-leverage). It does not hand you a setup — every retail-timeframe "trade the manipulation" rule we examine dies on hindsight labeling, crowding, and turnover cost.
