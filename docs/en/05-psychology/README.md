# 05 · Trading Psychology

The behavioral side of markets — biases, emotions, and sentiment gauges — tested against the one question that matters: does any of it *predict price*, or does it only help you avoid sabotaging yourself?

| Topic | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| [Cognitive Biases](cognitive-biases.md) | ⚠️ | Not a signal — there is nothing to backtest; genuinely useful for discipline and risk, not prediction. |
| [Fear & Greed Index](fear-and-greed-index.md) | ❌ | Lagging and largely circular (greed *is* recent returns); thresholds are tunable and it doesn't beat placebo as a signal. |

The honest framing: **psychology is risk management, not alpha.** Knowing your biases keeps you from sabotaging the real edges you have — for example, from cutting short the rare big winners that [trend following](../03-strategies/trend-following.md) depends on — but it does not predict price. Use it defensively; do not mistake it for a signal.
