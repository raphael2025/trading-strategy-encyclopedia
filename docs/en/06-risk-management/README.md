# 06 · Risk Management

How to control risk honestly — tested the same way as the strategies: causal timestamping, costs internalized, and a sharp eye for the difference between what *feels* safe and what *is* safe.

| Topic | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| [Position Sizing & Vol-Targeting](position-sizing-and-vol-targeting.md) | ✅ | Drawdown is a near-linear dial — halve size, halve drawdown, while Sharpe/Calmar stay ~invariant. An identity about how risk scales; nothing to overfit. |
| [Stop-Losses](stop-losses.md) | ❌ / 🔶 | Tight stops kill the payoff ratio the edge depends on; wide ATR trailing helps as catastrophe insurance. The verdict splits on width. |
| [Leverage](leverage.md) | ❌ | A bankruptcy switch, not a multiplier — geometric drag and absorbing liquidation mean negative expected growth at high leverage. Stay ≤1×. |

Risk management is the one place where retail can actually move the needle — but the lever is **size**, not clever exits and not leverage. Control the *scale* of your bets and let the validated edge breathe.
